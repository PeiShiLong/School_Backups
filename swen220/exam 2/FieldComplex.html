Alloy Dynamic Model Exam
Sports Field Complex Scheduler

Overview

For this exam you will complete a dynamic model skeleton. The problem is that of scheduling games at a sports field complex without conflicts. 

Descritpion of the starting files:

FieldComplex_static.als
The original static model, with three facts constraining the legal solutions. While you don't have to consult this - everything you need is in the dynamic solution skeleton - it may help you get a feel for how the scheduling system works.

FieldComplex_dynamic.als
The transformed static model, with an ordered Step set, relations modified to have a Step column, facts changed to step-variant predicates, and the Invariant and init predicates defined for you. There are skeletons for the  operation predicates to schedule, cancel and reschedule games. The closure assertions and existence predicates are provided for you.

FieldComplex.thm
A theme that may help you in visualizing solutions.

Problem

Your task it to fill in the init predicate and the operation predicates scheduleGame, cancelGame, and rescheduleGame. Comments at the head of each predicate specify what is to be done. Places where you are to provide your statements are marked

// Fill This In

Of course the results must be consistent with the provided closure assertions and the existence predicates as well.

Note: the operation predicates have a let statement defining the next step in comments - this is so you can develop and test each component separately without syntax errors. When you start work on an operation predicate, remove the comments.

The the scheduleGame and cancelGame predicates are each worth 12 points, and rescheduleGame is worth 6 points, for a total of 30 points.

Deliverables
