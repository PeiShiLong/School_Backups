SWEN-220 Alloy Exam #1

Museum Model

Rules of the Game

Your instructor will tell you how to download the files needed for this exam.

You are to model a simple museum that loans some of its collection (at a hefty price) to individual and corporate clients. The model uses the predefined signatures and relations in file Museum.als; a theme that makes visualization a bit easier is in Museum.thm.

The signatures, relations are documented, as are the facts you are to complete. If you have any questions about interpreting what is required, ask your instructor.

You are NOT to:

  • Add or modify any signatures or relations (fields),
  • Add any of your own facts. (You may optionally use predicates and/or assertions in developing your model, but remove them  before submitting your final solution.)

Brief Model Description

The signatures in the model are:

  • Picture - pictures in the museum's collection.
  • Client - individuals and corporations that can have pictures on loaned, and can express the desire for other pictures.
  • The one and only Museum, that tracks all the pictures on display, including some in the permanent collection that can never be loaned.

The facts are designed to ensure a consistent state, which means (among other things):

  • Pictures in the permanent collection are always on display.
  • All pictures are either on display or on loan to a client; no picture can be simultaneously on display and on loan.
  • No two clients can have the same picture loaned to them.
  • If a client desires a picture, it must be on loan (otherwise the museum could loan the picture immediately to the client).
  • No client can desire a picture that the client already has on loan.

Grading

  • Each fact is worth 5 points. 
  • Partial credit will be considered. Use comments to describe the expression you are trying to write.
  • Make sure that your model is free of syntax errors. Comment out those facts generating errors so that valid ones can execute.
  • Submit your solution to the Alloy Exam #1 dropbox in myCourses. Multiple solutions encouraged, last one graded.
